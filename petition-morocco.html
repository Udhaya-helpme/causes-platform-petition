Animal Welfare

Stop the mass killing of stray dogs ahead of Morocco's 2030 World Cup

Started by SPCA International · Addressed to: FIFA and the Government of Morocco · Active 2025–2026

As Morocco prepares to co-host the 2030 FIFA World Cup alongside Portugal and Spain, animal welfare organizations worldwide have raised alarm over reports of mass killings of stray dogs in Moroccan cities. Dogs are being shot, poisoned with strychnine, and beaten in public — often in front of children — as part of what authorities describe as a street clean-up ahead of the tournament.

An estimated 3 million stray dogs live in Morocco. Animal welfare groups warn that if the current cull continues at this rate, the vast majority of these animals could be killed before the World Cup begins. In June 2025, a PETA activist invaded the pitch during a FIFA Club World Cup match holding a sign reading "Morocco: stop shooting dogs and cats."

What we're asking for

This petition calls on FIFA to publicly condemn Morocco's culling practices and enforce its own animal welfare commitments. When Morocco was awarded the hosting rights, it formally committed to prohibiting animal culling. That commitment has not been honoured. FIFA has the power — and the responsibility — to hold its host nations accountable.

We also call on the Moroccan government to immediately halt the killings and fully implement the Trap-Neuter-Vaccinate-Return program it agreed to in 2019. Humane solutions exist. Mass culling is not only cruel — it is scientifically ineffective at controlling stray populations long-term.

Why this matters now

Over 80 nonprofits have joined the International Animal Welfare Protection Coalition calling for action. Jane Goodall has written to FIFA expressing being "absolutely appalled." The petition has gathered over 74,000 signatures globally. No sporting event should come at the cost of millions of animal lives.
