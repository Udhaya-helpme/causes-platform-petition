Animal Welfare

Save Dartmoor's hill ponies from extinction

Started by Friends of the Dartmoor Hill Pony · Addressed to: UK Secretary of State for Environment · Active 2026

Dartmoor's semi-wild hill ponies have roamed the moor for thousands of years. Today, fewer than 1,000 remain — already classified as endangered by the United Nations. Now, new grazing contracts drawn up by Natural England could wipe out up to 90% of what is left, potentially before the end of 2026.

Under the new contracts, ponies are counted alongside cattle and sheep in total livestock quotas. Since ponies are far less commercially valuable than cattle, farmers will be forced to remove them to stay within the limits. The most likely outcome is a mass cull during the autumn drift season, when ponies are brought off the moor for welfare checks.

What we're asking for

This petition calls on the UK Secretary of State for Environment to intervene immediately — ordering a pause on the new contracts and an independent review before they come into force. Dartmoor's ponies are not just iconic; they are ecologically vital. Research shows they are one of the few animals that eat molinia, an invasive grass threatening the moor's biodiversity. Losing them would accelerate environmental decline, not reverse it.

Politicians across party lines have spoken out. The Prime Minister has stated the government will not support a cull. But statements are not enough — we need binding protection written into the contracts before the October drifts.

Why this matters now

Over 200,000 people have signed petitions to save the Dartmoor hill pony, with the Friends charity delivering signatures to Downing Street. The clock is ticking. Once these animals are removed from the moor, the genetic population may be too small to recover. Add your name and share — this is one of the most urgent native breed conservation crises in the UK right now.
