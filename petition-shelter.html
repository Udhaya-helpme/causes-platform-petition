Animal Welfare

Fund mobility aids for shelter dogs with disabilities

Started by Maya Okonkwo · Addressed to: City Animal Services Departments · Started June 2026

Every year, thousands of dogs with mobility impairments enter public shelters with little hope of adoption. The primary barrier is cost — mobility aids like wheelchairs and harnesses can run $200-$600 per dog, a price most adopters cannot absorb on top of standard adoption fees.

The result is that these dogs spend far longer in shelters, experiencing prolonged stress, and ultimately facing higher euthanasia rates than their able-bodied peers.

What we're asking for

This petition calls on city and county animal services departments to establish a dedicated mobility aid fund — budgeted annually — covering wheelchairs, harnesses, and physical therapy referrals for dogs in public shelters.

Cities that have piloted this have seen adoption rates for mobility-aided dogs increase by over 60%. This works. We are asking for it to be funded systematically, not left to individual shelter fundraising.
